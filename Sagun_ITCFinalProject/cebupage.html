DESCRIPTION

Cebu is a Philippine province that encompasses Cebu Island as well as many other nearby islands. Its name has a number of different meanings, but according to legend, it was derived from the word "Sibu," which means "trade." The island has since become an important trading center where various products such as agricultural products, precious stones, perfumes, and other items are bartered during Rajah Humabon's reign. Its harbors became known as "Sinibuayng Hingpit," which translates to "the place for trading," and was later shortened to sibu or sibo ("to trade"), from which the modern name "Cebu" was derived.
