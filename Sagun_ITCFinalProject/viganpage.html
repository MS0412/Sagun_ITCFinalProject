VIGAN CITY

ALL ABOUT VIGAN

DESCRIPTION

The historic town of Vigan was founded in the late 1500s, during the Spanish dominance of the Philippines, and has retained its appearance and feel over the centuries. The well-preserved city is the capital of Ilocos Sur, a province in northwest Luzon, and a UNESCO World Heritage site.
The development of a unique townscape and culture, with no parallel anywhere else in Southeast Asia, resulted from the unique architectural fusion of colonial Spanish and Philippine building designs. It gets its name from the word 'Biga,' which refers to a large taro plant that grows abundantly along the Mestizo River's banks.
